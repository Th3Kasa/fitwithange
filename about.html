01 / 04 — Origin

The Story

Built With Purpose

Ange graduated with a Bachelor of Exercise Physiology from the University of Sydney — a degree that is not a weekend certificate, but four years of anatomy, physiology, biomechanics, and clinical application. That foundation shapes every session, every programme, every conversation.

The fitness industry trades in insecurity. Extreme cuts, before-and-after culture, the constant suggestion that you are not enough as you are. FitWithAnge exists to counter all of it — a coaching practice built on respect, evidence, and the conviction that women deserve better than generic plans written for generic bodies.

Strength is not a look. It is a capacity — and it compounds.

Working both online and face-to-face at Anytime Fitness Martin Place in Sydney’s CBD, Ange coaches women at every stage — from first-time lifters to experienced athletes looking for a smarter approach. The programme is called Built With Purpose for a reason: nothing is arbitrary.

02 / 04 — Method

Every programme is written from scratch. Periodised. Explained. Adjusted as life shifts around it. The goal is not transformation for transformation’s sake — it is building a body that performs, recovers, and feels genuinely yours.

How I Coach

Four principles.
Every session.

These are not values on a wall. They are the actual structure of how the work gets done.

01

Strength First

Progressive overload sits at the centre of every programme. Muscle is the metabolic asset most women are told to fear — building it changes body composition, bone density, and the way you move through the world.

02

Sustainable Over Extreme

The most effective plan is the one you can actually maintain. No crash phases, no unsustainable deficits. Everything is calibrated to integrate with a full life — not replace it.

03

Education & Autonomy

Every prescription comes with an explanation. Understanding the why behind your training means you can make intelligent decisions long after the programme ends. That’s the actual goal.

04

Whole-Person Approach

Sleep quality, hormonal fluctuations, stress load, life season — all of it affects adaptation. Coaching that ignores context is just a spreadsheet. This practice considers the full picture.
